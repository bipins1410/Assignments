FAQ for Cancer disease

  1. What is cancer?

    Answer

    Cancer is when cells in the body change and grow out of control. Your body is made up of tiny building blocks called cells.
    Normal cells grow when your body needs them, and die when your body does not need them any longer.
    Cancer is made up of abnormal cells that grow even though your body does not need them. In most types of cancer, the abnormal cells grow to form a lump or mass called a tumor.

  2. What causes cancer?

    Answer

    Two types of factors contribute to the cause of cancer. One is a tendency or predisposition to develop cancer.
    The other is exposure to the triggers that start it off. Examples are cigarettes, environmental toxins, sun exposure, or liver damage.

  3. Are chemicals and pollutants causing cancer?

    Answer

    In a very small portion of special cases only. In certain jobs, prolonged exposure to a few chemicals may cause certain rare kinds of cancers.
    Nowadays almost all of these substances have been identified and are regulated.

  4. Does cigarette smoke really cause cancer?

    Answer

    Yes. Cigarettes cause the vast majority of cancers of the lung. They are a major factor in cancers of the bladder, pancreas, mouth, larynx, esophagus, and kidney.

  5. Can attitudes or stress cause cancer?

    Answer

    Not as far as researchers know. In fact, the idea that the cancer “personality” or a bad attitude contributes to the cause of cancer may be part of the ancient human habit of blaming the patient for the disease.

  6. Will my hair grow back?

    Answer

    Yes, hair loss from chemotherapy is temporary. It will grow back, usually after therapy is finished. In some cases, hair can grow back during therapy.
    Usually the texture of the hair is different for the first year. Then, after a year or so, it usually goes back to how it was before you took chemotherapy.
    Hair loss from radiation therapy may be irreversible.
